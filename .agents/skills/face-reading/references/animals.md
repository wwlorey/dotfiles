# The 36 Animal Archetypes (Prosopa's typology)

A distinct personality typology Prosopa runs alongside (and sometimes instead of) the planetary archetypes. Drawn from his three-part series: "The Twelve Animal Personalities," "Twelve MORE Animal Personality Archetypes," and "The Twelve RAREST Animal Personalities."

His mechanism claim: shared mammalian biochemistry + hormones + selection pressures mean a human who *looks* like an animal often *behaves* like it. The Belyaev fox-domestication experiment (selecting for tameness produced floppy ears, piebald coats, raised tails) is his recurring proof.

Use in Step 3 alongside the planetary archetype, or in Step 6 as a closing pattern-name. Prosopa pairs them — Mars face + lion archetype = bold/regal; Sun face + owl archetype = aloof/wise.

## Tier 1 — The Original Twelve

### Bird
- **Cues**: sloping side profile, narrow face, long prominent nose, big bulging eyes
- **Traits**: sensitive, flighty, talkative, witty; nervous, quick; "mercurial Gemini energy"
- **Temperament**: melancholic
- *"Mercury face" overlap*

### Pig
- **Cues**: heavy-set, short upturned nose, deep-set beady eyes (cynicism, distrust), wide mouth
- **Traits**: hedonistic, greedy, gluttonous; pleasure-seekers — food, partying, alcohol, sex

### Bulldog
- **Cues**: broad / robust head, short nose, big jaws, aggressive flat or arched eyebrows
- **Traits**: impulsive, violent, dominating; "a lot of criminals, especially of the violent variety, have this"
- **Worked example**: Winston Churchill

### Frog / Bullfrog
- **Cues**: watery, full body; soft, pale; bulging eyes, wide mouth (full or thin lips)
- **Traits**: eccentric, quirky, creative, emotional, "big talkers," jokesters
- **Temperament**: phlegmatic

### Deer / Doe
- **Cues**: big wide-set eyes, narrow face, small mouth, flat nose against the face
- **Traits**: gentle, herbivorous, fearful, cautious, kind, withdrawn

### Horse
- **Cues**: long narrow face, steady focused eyes, strong robust features, tall strong body (sometimes lanky with visible muscle), strong chin, often long hair
- **Traits**: enduring, mobile, freedom-loving; gym/outdoors type

### Fox
- **Cues**: sharp face, sharp eyes, angular brows, pointy ear tops, long pointy nose
- **Traits**: witty, clever, intelligent, deep thinker, somewhat predatory
- **Worked example**: Tom Hiddleston

### Rat
- **Cues**: narrow face, recessed chin, recessed/slanting forehead, tiny beady eyes, big teeth
- **Traits**: distrustful, cynical, "they think other people are out to get them so they're out to get other people"; trickster

### Cat
- **Cues**: round / heart-shaped face (Venus shape), pointy triangular ears, short button nose, positive canthal tilt
- **Traits**: assertive, rebellious, aggressive, confident; "natural seducers and seductresses"; sleek, independent, moves "in the shadows"
- **Worked example**: Hannibal Lecter (Anthony Hopkins portrayal)

### Wolf
- **Cues**: longer narrow face, long prominent pointed nose, strong chin (no bird impression), deep-set eyes, big mouth with prominent teeth
- **Traits**: aggressive, takes what they want by force
- **Worked example**: AOC ("big teeth and that overall face... a lupine demeanor"); Conor McGregor

### Lion
- **Cues**: broad jaw, thick hair and beard, deep-set eyes, big mouth and teeth, thick mane (curly/wavy blonde is ideal)
- **Traits**: high testosterone; courageous, bold, vicious-but-noble, kingly, regal
- *Jupiter **face shape** overlap (broad reverse-trapezoid), not Jupiter **archetype** — Lion is choleric/sanguine where Jupiter-the-clergyman is phlegmatic. Same morphology, different temperament-fit.*

### Eagle / Vulture (bird-of-prey)
- **Cues**: sharp deep-set eyes, prominent hooked nose
- **Traits**: same morphology — moral valence depends on the person. **Eagle** = glorious leader; **vulture** = scavenger / parasite
- **Worked example**: Donald Trump ("much more of an eagle's look. A bird of prey looking down at the world and then swooping down")

## Tier 2 — The Next Twelve

### Mouse
- **Cues**: rounded, small features; small / petite frame; short stature
- **Traits**: humble, meek, shy, quiet, conformist; genuine, good-hearted; neurotic, very sensitive, giving; prey-type
- *"Whereas the rat is sneaky and sleazy, the mouse is genuine and good-hearted."*

### Beaver
- **Cues**: square face, very steady, angular features; bigger / huskier than the mouse
- **Traits**: steady, reliable, level-headed, diligent, workaholic; downside — practical to the point of dull, overbearing
- **MBTI overlay**: classic ISTJ

### Prairie Dog
- **Cues**: round, soft features; approachable, non-threatening; a bit heavy-set, rounded
- **Traits**: patient, enduring, tolerant, visionary; warm, social, cuddly; strong need for family/friends; caretaking; "best wives and mothers"
- **Temperament**: phlegmatic
- **MBTI overlay**: ESFJ

### Elephant
- **Cues**: big head, big face, big personality; distinct, can't-be-ignored look; tall, large — heavy-set or muscular
- **Traits**: patriarchs / matriarchs; kind, sensitive, empathetic; strong, confident, bold; herbivore protector; "wants to destroy bullies, create justice and harmony"; institution-builder
- **MBTI overlay**: ENFJ

### Tiger
- **Cues**: strongly positive canthal tilt; broader head and face; big mouth; thin lips; deep-set eyes; very inexpressive
- **Traits**: killer instinct; driven, aggressive, independent; "sigma to the lion's alpha"; doesn't care about pleasing others; succeeds as CEOs, politicians, military leaders
- **Worked example**: Hillary Clinton ("a real tiger of a woman")

### Goat
- **Cues**: mix of eccentric and practical features; often facial hair (satyr-like)
- **Traits**: grounded pragmatism + eccentric oddness; hard-headed, stubborn, ornery; cannot be told what to do; exploratory, omnivorous; restless wanderer
- **Worked example**: Kanye West ("Yay... the goat himself... restless wanderer, looking to butt heads with people")

### Badger
- **Cues**: very pointed, bullet-like side profile; mid-face projects forward; big protruding nose pointing out
- **Traits**: DGAF; natural burrower / homemaker; strong appetites — food, wealth, safety, power, lovers, security; willful, principled, driven, aggressive; "wants own fortress, big house with land, gun safe"
- **Hybrid**: prairie dog (burrowing) + pig (appetite) + wolf (intensity)

### Bat (fringe / nocturnal)
- **Cues**: big eyes, big ears, big inner face; small-bodied like a mouse; tends to favor dark baggy clothing
- **Traits**: shy, eccentric, artsy, awkward; sensitive; good in the arts
- **Hybrid**: bird + mouse + a bit of rat
- **Worked example**: Billie Eilish

### Hyena (fringe)
- **Cues**: big mouth, big teeth, big chin; oddly long face
- **Traits**: wolf aggression + rat shameless sleaze + fox trickiness + cat/tiger killer instinct; strong appetites and libido; usually proles or elites, rarely middle class; high-profile conspicuous criminals; shameless social strivers
- **Worked example**: The Joker, Deadpool (fictional); Kamala Harris; Jeffrey Epstein; P. Diddy

### Skunk (fringe)
- **Cues**: aggressively off-putting styling — tattoos, piercings, weird makeup; uses appearance as warning coloration
- **Traits**: sensitive, thoughtful, neurotic; uses weirdness / eccentricity / aggressive oddness as defense mechanism against "normies and would-be predators"
- **Worked example**: Marilyn Manson
- *Would otherwise be a bat, mouse, fox, or bird if healthier*

### Snake (fringe)
- **Cues**: wide mouth, thin lips (strong appetite); very beady, deep-set eyes; very robust features; broad head
- **Traits**: true cold-blooded predator; "natural-born killer"; highly valued by Marines / special forces because no compunction about killing; otherwise becomes serial killer; drive is literally to kill
- *"One of the few non-mammals" in the system*
- **Worked examples**: Jeffrey Dahmer, Ted Bundy, Richard Ramirez

### Slug (fringe)
- **Cues**: none — the slug is defined by *absence of drive*, not appearance
- **Traits**: made, not born; lost all passion; rank hedonism, consuming, passivity, laziness; "doom scrollers, bed rotters, rumers, doomers, cumers, gooners"; overstimulation until they become nonentities

## Tier 3 — The Rarest Twelve

### Owl
- **Cues**: big round eyes; round faces; high-set eyebrows; wider eyes
- **Traits**: wise, circumspect, insightful, poetic, focused; aloof, aristocratic, almost arrogant; "above it all"; nocturnal; very high standards; Athena's symbol

### Ostrich
- **Cues**: longer face with wide-set eyes; tall, lanky, gangly
- **Traits**: avian anxiety + equine confidence and physical energy; awkward but in a charming, appealing way; wacky, eccentric
- **Hybrid**: bird + horse

### Vulture (extended from Tier 1)
- **Cues**: bald head (men) or thinning hair (women); long hooked nose; longer face; heavier phlegmatic body
- **Traits**: ultimate opportunists; swoops in on opportunities; doesn't care for moral scruples; sharp, quick wit; physically slow, calm, detached, stress-resistant
- **Temperament**: phlegmatic

### Rooster (a.k.a. Peacock)
- **Cues**: pointier face; melancholic side profile
- **Traits**: nervous, quick, flighty, insecure; needs outside validation; overcompensates via dressy appearance; "birds trying to be lions"; aggressive peacocking displays (mostly male phenomenon)
- **Temperament**: melancholic

### Penguin
- **Cues**: sometimes longer, sometimes shorter face; maybe darker hair; long nose; deep-set eyes; quite heavy body
- **Traits**: heavy, stable, grounded yet sensitive and nitpicky; avian wit focused on picking out details; "good in the world of cinema as producers and directors because they can sit and focus without flying off after their own ideas"

### Bull
- **Cues**: broad, square, lower face; deep-set eyes; big, strong body
- **Traits**: stubborn, aggressive, combative ("Taurean"); very practical, grounded, focused on tangible things, here-and-now; "ultimate pragmatist in an aggressive, driven way"
- *Sits between ungulates (horse / pig / elephant) and predators (wolf / tiger / lion)*

### Bear
- **Cues**: big face; smaller, deep-set eyes; little small mouth; endomorphic body — big, less muscular, more fat; "fat but strong"
- **Traits**: lovable, huggable, cuddly; family-oriented, loyal; at the same time driven, aggressive, hungry, avaricious; "wants to make their den and protect their cubs"
- *Differentiated from badger by sheer confidence and assertiveness*

### Coyote
- **Cues**: narrow face, beady eyes, big teeth, pointy ears; a little physically bigger than rat / fox; "less brain, more brawn"
- **Traits**: wolf strength + fox sharpness + rat / hyena sleaziness; predator / scavenger; more direct, aggressive, blunt than rat / fox; not as confident as a true wolf, not as refined as a fox

### Golden Retriever
- **Cues**: maybe a longer face; very kind, soft features; often blonde in Caucasian populations
- **Traits**: confident, kind, soft, open, giving, generous; **not** pushovers — will stand up for what's right and for friends / family; great actors and public faces; "golden retriever energy"

### Monkey
- **Cues**: big ears (independence in physiognomy); nice wide mouth (talkativeness); big cheery cheeks; classic wide-faced look
- **Traits**: lovable, charismatic, great in front of a camera; independent, driven; "cleverest, smartest animal"
- **Worked examples**: Shad White; Barack Obama

### Lizard
- **Cues**: bug-eyed, bony-faced, gaunt-cheeked
- **Traits**: awkward; quick, flighty, reactive; bird-like energy with more coldness and detachment; "seem a little alien at times"
- **Worked example**: Steve Buscemi (reclassified from rat to lizard)

### Dragon (the 36th — mythical)
- **Cues**: very square, broad face; extremely sharp jawline; gaunt cheeks; bony cheekbones; very distinct, marked look; not a lot of facial fat; very intense, direct laser-focused gaze
- **Traits**: aggressive, driven, mythical; once-in-a-generation figures; "captivate the world with their energy, aura, charisma, and drive"
- *The only mythological creature, "saved for last"*

## How to use the animal layer

- Name an animal archetype in Step 3 alongside the planetary archetype, or in Step 6 (synthesis) as a closing image.
- Hybrid is natural — Prosopa builds badger as prairie dog + pig + wolf, ostrich as bird + horse. Name the hybrid components.
- Pair the animal with the temperament and the planetary archetype: "Lion-Jupiter-choleric" or "Cat-Venus-sanguine" is the full character signature.
- Worked examples Prosopa cites are useful comparison points — name them when the resemblance is strong.
- The animal layer often resolves ambiguity in the planetary call: a face between Mars and Saturn might read clearly as wolf or coyote.
