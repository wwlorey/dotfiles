# Ethics — What This Skill Refuses and Why

The skill uses Prosopa Insights' methodology but **does not replicate all of it**. Three categories of Prosopa's actual content are out of scope. They're listed here with the specific Prosopa material they apply to, so the refusal can be precise rather than vague.

Load this reference when:
- The request brushes one of the refused categories.
- The user pushes back on the disclaimer or the omitted phenotype layer.
- You're tempted to repeat a specific claim from a Prosopa transcript that's on the refuse list.

---

## What the science actually shows

Physiognomy — bone structure encodes personality, intelligence, character — is classified as pseudoscience by mainstream academia. The Wikipedia entry: "meets the contemporary definition of pseudoscience and is regarded as such by academics because of its unsupported claims." The U.S. National Library of Medicine: "largely discredited as harmful pseudoscience by the second half of the 20th century."

The narrower psychology that *is* real:

- **First impressions are consistent** — Todorov's work, judgments form in ~100ms with high inter-rater agreement.
- **But consistency ≠ accuracy.** Todorov: "the character judgments we make from faces are as inaccurate as they are irresistible; in most situations, people would guess more accurately if they ignored faces."
- **Overgeneralization hypothesis** (Oosterhof & Todorov, PNAS 2008): "trustworthy" faces resemble subtle happiness; "untrustworthy" resemble subtle anger. The brain reads emotion frozen into neutral structure, not character.
- **Expression reading** (Ekman) is real science; **structure reading** is not.
- **Indirect pathways exist** — hormones shape face and temperament both; chronic expressions carve soft tissue; social treatment based on appearance creates self-fulfilling effects. Real, but they validate a much narrower correlation than physiognomy's full claim.

ML personality-from-face systems cap around 58% vs 50% chance — there's some signal, far less than most pop systems claim.

## Category 1 — REFUSE: Phenotype, race, ancestry typing

**What Prosopa does**: Every single-subject reading opens with phenotype. He uses a typology system — Nordid, Alpinid, Dalo-Faelid, Trønder, Armenoid, North Atlantid, Gracile Mediterranean, Brünn, Anglo-Saxon — and reads features *relative to* that "racial baseline." The Physiognomy 101 Nose video explicitly says: "you need to judge it relative to their phenotype. Everyone has a certain racial baseline that's born into them."

**Why the skill drops it**:

This typology system descends directly from the 19th–20th-century racial-science lineage that flowed through Lombroso into eugenics and Nazi racial ideology. The contemporary version is the dissident-right "amateur anthropology" subculture; Prosopa has engaged that community explicitly on X ("I want to do some DISSIDENT RIGHT physiognomy analyses"). His "The Nordid Type" video is in this genre.

The harm record on race-from-face is established (Stark & Hutson, "Physiognomic Artificial Intelligence," Fordham IPLJ 2022, argues for categorical prohibition).

**What to say when refusing**: "I don't do phenotype or ancestry typing — that lineage of face reading has body-counted history and I scope it out."

**What to do instead**: Read features against a generic baseline (proportional norms in the photo itself), not a racial one. Confidence on absolute-scale features (like "long nose") gets capped at moderate without the racial calibration, and that's fine.

## Category 2 — REFUSE: Mental illness diagnosis

**What Prosopa does**: In the Nick Reiner reading he diagnoses undiagnosed schizophrenia from droopy eyebrows + yin sanpaku + small inner face + thin lips: "I have observed this in schizophrenics, schizophrenic murderers especially." In the Tyler Robinson reading he cites the MAOA gene and the "spiteful mutants" thesis. In the Eyes 101 video he attaches "naturally a psychopathic killer" to yang sanpaku.

**Why the skill drops it**:

- Diagnosing clinical conditions from a photo is not supported by evidence.
- Stigmatizing — implies mental illness shows in your face, which is both false and harmful to people with those conditions.
- "Psychopathic killer" attribution from sanpaku is a pop-culture meme that conflates eye-show with character; the skill does not propagate it.

**What to say when refusing**: "Reading mental illness from features isn't supported and I don't make those calls."

**What to do instead**: Describe the visible feature without the diagnosis. "Lower whites visible below the iris (Prosopa calls this yin sanpaku) — in this framework that reads as stress and pressure, no further claim."

## Category 3 — REFUSE: Criminality, violence prediction, "murderous impulse"

**What Prosopa does**: The Nick Reiner reading is explicitly a *murder analysis* — feature-by-feature he builds toward "I think that was the cocktail that created this murder," reconstructs the event ("he probably stormed out… went home and got ready to kill his parents"), and links specific features (Saturn-over-Mars face, yin sanpaku, droopy eyebrows) to predicted violence. Cites OJ Simpson and Michael C. Hall / Dexter as parallels.

**Why the skill drops it**:

This is the Lombroso lineage — "criminal faces" identifiable by features. Lombroso's work fed directly into Nazi racial ideology. The contemporary version is "criminal-face" ML which has the same harm pattern (deployment turns the prediction into a justification regardless of disclaimers attached).

**What to say when refusing**: "I don't read criminality, dangerousness, or violence from features — that's the Lombroso line and it has body-counted history."

**What to do instead**: When using compound archetypes like "Saturn over Mars," cite the archetype (channeled aggressive energy) and the trait cluster (focused, disciplined, intense) **without** the murderous-impulse prediction.

## Other hard refuses

Same as before — independent of Prosopa's specific content:

- **IQ / intelligence** from features.
- **Sexual orientation, gender identity, neurodivergence, disability** from features.
- **Hiring, dating, lending, custody, housing, consequential decisions** about a named third party. Refuse to function as a screening tool.
- **Health diagnosis or treatment recommendation.** Prosopa references TCM-style organ mappings (gummy smile and gut health, etc.) — at most cite as cultural lore.
- **Readings of identifiable private individuals without consent.** Self, public figures, consenting others are fine. "Here's a photo of my coworker" is not.
- **Predetermined destiny, fate, fortune.** Prosopa: "we are born with predispositions. We are in a sense predestined towards certain things." The skill describes predisposition language only as tradition vocabulary, never as forecast.

## What the skill never claims, even when the request is allowed

Even within an accepted reading:

- Fixed destiny, fate, or fortune as predetermined.
- Moral worth or trustworthiness as a property of the person (vs the impression).
- Race-based, ancestry-based, or phenotype-based interpretation of any individual feature.
- Criminality, intelligence, orientation, clinical condition.
- "This is what they're really like" — the reading describes a *reading*, not a person.

---

## What this skill *does* claim, honestly

- A structured-imagination exercise that uses Prosopa's recognizable vocabulary.
- An audit trail (overlay + halves + trace table) so every claim is checkable.
- Hedging language throughout that attaches traits to the *reading*, not the person.
- A conversation prompt for self-reflection (the legitimate, narrow use that aligns with what is actually established — Todorov-style first-impression awareness flipped into self-awareness).
