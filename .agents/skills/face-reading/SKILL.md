# Face Reading (Prosopa Insights style)

Take a face image, produce a Taylor G. Northcutt-style reading: temperament from the side profile, planetary AND animal archetype from the face shape, feature-by-feature traits walked top-down, and a left/right asymmetry pass with mirrored half-face composites. Every reading ships with an annotated overlay image (what was measured), the two half-face composites (so the user can see the inner/outer split for themselves), and a trace table (every claim with its confidence).

All methodology and vocabulary comes from Prosopa's own YouTube videos.

## Two-layer model

Prosopa frames physiognomy as a two-layer system, repeated across his channel:

- **Temperament** — inborn, unchanging; bodily; read from the side profile + body. The four classical humors (choleric / sanguine / phlegmatic / melancholic).
- **Personality** — the persona / mask the temperament builds over life. "Persona" = Latin for mask. Read from the face shape (planetary archetype + animal archetype) and individual features.

His causal claim: genes pleiotropically code for both physical features and mental traits; epigenetics (childhood trauma, life events) toggles expression; hormones (testosterone, estrogen) shape morphology and behavior simultaneously. "You are your body." This is the implicit grounding — name it only if asked.

## Inputs (preflight checklist)

Address each item before reading. Do not proceed to Step 1 until every line is either acquired or explicitly waived in the output with a stated reason.

- [ ] **Front-facing photo, neutral expression, head level, good lighting.** Smiling distorts mouth shape, lip line, and cheek volume.
- [ ] **Side profile.** Prosopa's temperament call depends on it — forehead slope, chin projection, jaw line angle, "bow of melancholy" vs flat vs concave curve. Without one, infer cautiously from front-view cues and flag temperament confidence as moderate or lower.
- [ ] **Smile photo, for known subjects — non-skippable.** Gummy smile, tooth proportions, tooth gap, and lip-corner shape are load-bearing when present and the neutral photo may not capture them. For any subject with public photos available, pull one.
- [ ] **Youth photo, for older subjects.** Aging trajectory (Venus → Mars, Saturn → Sun) and the original face-shape archetype both depend on the younger face.

If the photo set is poor, name the cues you can't read confidently and proceed only with the rest.

## Reading procedure

Follow Prosopa's actual order. Each step gets a paragraph in the output.

### The headline finding

Before walking the rubric, look for the one observation that *defines* this face — the small inner face, the asymmetric two-character split, the predator-disguised-as-prey, the persistent Mercury-Saturn optical illusion, the bow of melancholy. Name it. Step 7 (synthesis) is built around it; the rubric walks are evidence in its support. A reading that walks all seven steps in equal weight without ever naming the load-bearing observation is the most common failure mode.

**Calibrate toward extremes, not midpoints.** Prosopa rarely calls a feature "moderate" — he picks an end of the spectrum (flat or splayed, deep or protruding, thin or full) and reads accordingly, or skips the cue. A "moderate" call usually means you haven't looked hard enough. Either commit to an end or drop the row from the trace table.

### Step 1 — Phenotype

Prosopa opens every reading with phenotype. Identify the subject's phenotype using his **-id taxonomy** (Nordid, Alpinid, Mediterranid, plus sub-types and contact types). The diagnostic feature stack: skin/hair (low weight) → **skull shape (dolichocephalic vs brachycephalic, large vs small)** → nose → body.

Phenotype is the **calibration baseline** — every feature you read in later steps is interpreted *relative to* the phenotype. A "long nose" on a Gracile Mediterranid is unremarkable; the same nose on an Alpinid is a significant divergence. Without this baseline, you can't tell which features are individual signals vs collective traits of the population.

State the call openly with sub-type and any contact-type parents: "this reads as a North Atlantid — Atlanto-Mediterranid × Nordid contact type, Henry Cavill complexion." Mixed phenotypes are the norm — name the contributors.

Confidence on phenotype from a photo alone is rarely better than moderate (most diagnostic traits are skeletal). Hedge appropriately. Full taxonomy and worked examples in `references/phenotype.md`.

### Step 2 — Gestalt

Describe the whole face in one or two sentences. Note dominant zone of the **facial thirds** (Prosopa's framework — upper = intellectual; middle = social/emotional; lower = material/practical) and the **inner-vs-outer face ratio** (compartmentalization axis — small inner face = aloof/doublespeak; large inner face = social attunement; see `references/features.md`). Name the immediate impression.

### Step 3 — Temperament (from side profile)

Pick one of choleric, sanguine, phlegmatic, melancholic — or a mixed call if regions diverge (e.g. "choleric with a sanguine forehead"). Prosopa allows compound temperaments: forehead carries the intellect's humor; chin carries the **initial energy of action**; jaw carries the **long-term endurance energy of action**.

Add the side-profile curvature overlay: convex (bird/bullet) = "bow of melancholy" (sensitivity, reactivity, creativity); flat = balanced; concave ("moonman crescent") = dull, unperceptive, overbearing.

Full cues in `references/temperaments.md`.

### Step 4 — Personality archetype (face shape)

**Two archetype layers** — Prosopa runs both. Call both when they're legible.

**Planetary archetype**: pick one of **Mars** (warrior), **Saturn** (accountant), **Earth** (farmer/businessman), **Sun** (aloof aristocrat), **Jupiter** (clergyman/judge), **Venus** (romantic diplomat), **Mercury** (jester/magician), **Uranus** (radical/sex-focused). Compound forms are legitimate and often more accurate ("Saturn over Mars," "Earth + Jupiter = Regulator"). Shadow archetypes ("shadow Venus") and the aging trajectory (Venus → Mars as the face sags) also live here.

**Animal archetype**: when one of the 36 falls out cleanly, name it — bird, pig, bulldog, frog, deer, horse, fox, rat, cat, wolf, lion, eagle/vulture, mouse, beaver, prairie dog, elephant, tiger, goat, badger, bat, hyena, skunk, snake, slug, owl, ostrich, vulture, rooster/peacock, penguin, bull, bear, coyote, golden retriever, monkey, lizard, dragon. The animal layer often resolves planetary ambiguity ("between Mars and Saturn → reads clearly as wolf"). **Skip this layer when no animal falls out cleanly** — Prosopa often skips it himself and uses "wolf in sheep's clothing" or similar as a closing metaphor in Step 6 rather than as a diagnostic Step 4 call.

Note the **archetype ↔ temperament fit** — Prosopa reads misfit as a person who "lost the plot" or whose life forced a channeling. Mention fit/misfit in the synthesis. Also note the **job-fit** the archetype + temperament points to.

Full catalogs in `references/archetypes.md` (planetary) and `references/animals.md` (animal).

### Step 5 — Feature walkthrough (top-down)

Walk in Prosopa's order:

1. **Forehead** — height, slope, creases (heavy creases = "intellectual effort throughout life").
2. **Eyebrows** — shape (arched / straight / curved / droopy / Spock), thickness ("energy reserve"), length (premeditation), set height (low = snap judgment), lay direction.
3. **Eyes** — almond vs round (intellect vs morals/emotion lens); deep-set (calculating) vs protruding (off-the-cuff); canthal tilt (positive = confident/rebellious; negative = people-pleaser); yin/yang sanpaku; brow-to-eye gap; full upper eyelid (need for intimacy/connection).
4. **Ears** — set height (high = abstract intellectual interest); splay (wide = independent; close-set = dependent/conformist); upper/middle/lower third bulging (Prosopa's vertical-thirds-of-the-ear).
5. **Nose** — length (long = thoughtful; short = action-oriented); shape (Greek/straight = cool-headed; Roman/aquiline = conquering; Egyptian/evenly-curved = mystical/passionate); bridge bump (reactivity, snapping under stress); bridge width ("energy reserve" — wide = stamina; narrow = sensitivity).
6. **Mouth and lips** — upper lip (relationship with the outside world); lower lip (relationship with own inner world / sense of self); width (wide = ambition, social engagement); corners (upturned / downturned / **lipless** = executive leadership capacity); cupid's bow (sun upper lip = blunt/cold; defined = warmth).
7. **Philtrum** — depth ("energy reserve" — strong link between social-emotional and material life); width ("principles vs pleasure" — wide = hedonistic, narrow = moralistic); length (motor activity — qualified by Prosopa as uncertain).
8. **Chin** — covered under the side profile, but observe cleft chin ("main character syndrome"), narrow vs wide, projection.

For each: name what you observe, state Prosopa's interpretation, mark confidence (clear / moderate / ambiguous). Full feature catalog in `references/features.md`.

### Step 6 — Asymmetry (left vs right composites)

Prosopa's asymmetry doctrine: **left side = inner self / who they came into the world as / private**; **right side = outer self / who they became / professional mask / public**. The mechanism he claims: right brain (authentic, spontaneous) → left side of face; left brain (calculated persona) → right side. Repeated asymmetric expressions embed into the face over time.

Build the two halves with the script:

```bash
python3 scripts/halves.py <input_image> <midline_x> <output_dir>
```

`midline_x` is the integer pixel x-coordinate of the vertical face midline (centerline through nose tip / philtrum / chin tip). Determine it by inspecting the photo. The script writes `halves-left.png`, `halves-right.png`, and `halves-side-by-side.png` to `output_dir`.

In the reading text, describe what each composite shows. Patterns to look for:
- **"Predator disguised as prey" / "wolf in sheep's clothing"** — the halves look like two different characters (Sydney Sweeney read).
- **Split face shape** — one half a pure planetary archetype, the other half a pure different archetype, no mixing (Mark Carney = pure Saturn right / pure Mars left).
- **Asymmetric features**: canthal tilt (positive left + negative right = privately driven, publicly conciliatory), eyelid fullness (left fuller = wants to overshare), eyebrow shape (right curved over time = public persona softened), worry lines (right = professional stress, left = personal stress, bilateral = family-business).
- **Convergence** — halves match → inner and outer are aligned. That's a finding too.

**Calibrate magnitude before naming a pattern.** Pixel-mirroring visually amplifies asymmetry — a modest "different modes for different parts of life" split can look like a two-character predator/prey divergence in the composite. Before naming "wolf in sheep's clothing," "split face shape," or "predator disguised as prey," verify with a second photo (different expression, angle, or lighting), or with the original side-by-side face. The halves composite is a hypothesis generator, not a verdict. When in doubt, name the *direction* of the split without dramatizing its *magnitude*.

### Step 7 — Synthesis

One paragraph, **built around the headline finding named at the start**. Pull temperament + planetary archetype + animal archetype + dominant features + asymmetry into a single character portrait that the headline finding ties together. Where Prosopa uses an animal archetype to close ("predator disguised as prey," "wolf in sheep's clothing"), name the pattern. Note where the archetype fits or misfits the temperament. Name the **job-fit / role-fit** the combination points to (CEO / VP / scholar / artist / clergy / etc.) if it's legible.

**Pair / counterpoint reading**: when the subject has a known partner (spouse, business partner, handler), Prosopa reads the two as functional complements — Epstein's small inner face (compartmentalized) ↔ Maxwell's large inner face (social attunement); Vance's phlegmatic-VP energy ↔ his choleric boss. If a partner is in scope, note the complementarity.

## Auditability artifacts

Three artifacts ship with every reading.

### A. Annotated overlay image

Gather your observations as JSON and call:

```bash
python3 scripts/overlay.py <input_image> <observations.json> <output_image>
```

Schema and example in `references/overlay-schema.md`. Draw the three facial thirds as boxes, jaw line, brow arch, eye-corner markers, philtrum endpoints, nose midline. Keep markers sparse.

### B. Half-face composites

From Step 5 above. Three images: `halves-left.png`, `halves-right.png`, `halves-side-by-side.png`. The side-by-side composite is the user-facing artifact; the individual halves are reference.

### C. Trace table

End the reading with a Markdown table — one row per substantive claim. Columns:

| Observation (what's in the pixels) | Prosopa concept | Claim (the trait) | Confidence |
|---|---|---|---|

`Prosopa concept` is e.g. "choleric forehead," "Saturn-over-Mars," "energy reserve (philtrum depth)," "left/right asymmetry doctrine," "lipless corners," "bow of melancholy." `Confidence`: clear / moderate / ambiguous. If a row would be low-confidence and high-stakes, drop it.

1. Phenotype (Step 1)
